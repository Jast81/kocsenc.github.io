Rate My Coop

Full Javascript tech stack
  • AngularJS Yeomen
  • NodeJS Loopback MariaDB
  • Fedora Grunt Nginx

One of my favorite software stories to tell. On a normal college day someone asks, "Why is there no tool to rate your coop?" Co-op culture at a college-wide level is rare; not at RIT. Because many majors require co-ops for graduation, it becomes way more serious. We could only find this this crappy website, but it sucks.

Craig and I looked at each other, paused, and said "Let's do it". It didn't take long before the nerd talk began; the scope of the project, the models and their relationships, the tech stack, the delivery date (which was about in 3 weeks).

The domain ratemycoop.io was purchased and the ball was rolling. Every night since then kind of felt like the scene from The Social Network where the developers are wired in just hammering things out (yes, even documentation) .

We had a strong prototype within hours, but if we wanted to release by career fair, we needed help. It didn't take long to find enthusiastic colleagues to add features and for a successful release.
Within days we had over 1000 sessions and hundreds of reviews.

Did I mention, we released it to the public?

Android Scraper

Data Analysis tech stack
  • Python Java R Bash
  • PostgreSQL
  • Red Hat Enterprise

The Android Scraper is a big data and computing intensive research project. Its a way to analyze a whopping 100,000+ apps (100's of TBs) to find out what features they have for data analysis. Features such as sharing to social media, SSL use, password encryption and more.

It consists of 2 main phases: Gathering and Analyzing
Gathering is all about finding efficient ways to break apart android apps through de-compilation and de-obfuscation to get source code.
Analyzing is all about finding robust ways to run static-analysis tools to figure out what features the apps have. Then, use data analysis to discover feature trends and impact.
